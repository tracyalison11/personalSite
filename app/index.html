About Me

I have traveled to many corners of the world but spent most of my life in front of a computer somewhere in California. I went to grade school in Southern California and middle school in Christchurch, New Zealand, where I learned a lot about diversity. I went to college at UC Santa Cruz and served as the president of the rugby club, where I learned a lot about teamwork. I went on to work at tech start-ups in Silicon Valley, where I learned a lot about the Internet of Things and fell in love with building products that people all over the world can use.

I taught myself how to code using a ton of different resources and learning from a lot of patient people. I still spend a large portion of my free time brushing up on my javascript skills and learning new frameworks. I love being a developer because it allows me to build, fix and learn new things all in a dayâ€™s work. I am also proud to have become a Women Who Code lead that hosts regular meet-ups for javascript and jQuery in an effort to introduce the joys of web development to new friends.

Outside of work my hobbies include travel, the great outdoors, rugby, yoga, reading and trying to take as many pictures of my dogs as possible.
